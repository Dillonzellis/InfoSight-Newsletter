Question and Answer

Q: Are there area restrictions for a multiple group FCU when adding a SEG?

A: Yes, the group to be added (regardless of size) must be within the federal credit unions service area (reasonable proximity to an existing service facility).

Service area is applicable only for multiple common bond credit unions. This is the area that can reasonably be served by the service facilities accessible to the groups within the field of membership. That is, the group to be added must be within reasonable proximity geographically to a service facility of the credit union.

Q: What are the application procedures for adding a new select group to our field of membership?

A: All new groups must be approved by NCUA. Requests for adding new groups can be made using the official Application for Field of Membership Amendment, either NCUA Form 4015-EZ (for groups of less than 3,000 people), Form 4015-A for groups between 3,000 and 4,999, or Form 4015 (for groups of 5,000 or more people).

Forms 4015-EZ, 4015-A, and 4015, along with procedures, can be found in NCUA Chartering and Field of Membership Manual of Federal Credit Unions.

If a credit union wants to add an occupational select group of less than 3,000 primary potential members, they have the option of filing an FOM Internet Application rather than the paper 4015-EZ form.

Application forms and procedures can also be easily found on NCUA's Website.

Q: What is NCUA's current position on permitting overlapping fields of membership?

A: In 2003, NCUA eliminated virtually all provisions that afford field of membership protection for a federal credit union in a particular area.

Overlaps involving groups of less than 3,000 are classified as incidental overlaps and no overlap analysis by the NCUA is required. Overlaps of 3,000 or more primary potential members require an overlap analysis. In their analysis, NCUA will consider factors such as the views of the overlapped credit union(s); financial effect on the overlapped credit unions; the desires of the group; whether the original credit union fails to provide requested services; the desire of the sponsor organization; and the best interests of the affected group and the credit union members involved.

Q: What is considered a “service facility” under the rules?

A: A service facility is a place where shares are accepted for members’ accounts, loan applications are accepted or loans are disbursed. This would include a credit union-owned branch, a mobile branch, an office operated on a regularly scheduled weekly basis, a credit union-owned ATM or a credit union owned electronic facility that meets the requirements. A service facility ALSO includes a shared branch or a shared branch network location, including a shared ATM or other electronic facility, if a credit union participates in a shared branching network.

However, for purposes of servicing an underserved area, the definition is different. A service facility for this purpose is a place where shares are accepted for members’ accounts, loan applications are accepted, and loans are disbursed. A service facility DOES NOT include an ATM or shared ATM.

Remember, the credit union’s internet website is not a service facility.
