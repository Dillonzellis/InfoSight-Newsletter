Questions and Answers

Q: Can the funds in a IRA or HSA that have been inactive for several years be escheated to the state as abandoned property?

A: Yes, according to a recent CUNA blog, “abandoned” IRA and HSA funds can be escheated to your state’s Unclaimed Property division.

Each state has its own Unclaimed Property law, likely based on the Uniform Unclaimed Property Act (UUPA) of 1995 – which was drafted by qualified lawyers to provide states “with non-partisan, well-conceived and well-drafted legislation, intended to bring clarity and stability to this area of law.” For example, in the case of an IRA account, the UUPA states that generally “an IRA can be considered abandoned if the IRA owner has not communicated an interest in the account after three years from when he or she was required to begin taking distributions.”

Credit unions must refer to their state’s law to determine how “abandoned” is defined, which could be more or less than the three years recommended in the UUPA. State law will likely have specific language regarding a credit union’s responsibilities for attempting to contact an IRA of HSA owner to determine if the account is truly abandoned. State law will dictate the specific steps that should be taken to start and complete the escheatment process. It may be beneficial to discuss proper procedures with your credit union’s legal counsel.

According to tax experts, the IRS has clarified that if a distribution of a portion or the entire amount of an individual’s IRA is paid to a state’s unclaimed property fund it is a reportable distribution in the name and Tax Identification Number of the IRA owner or beneficiary, even though the IRA owner or beneficiary does not benefit directly from the distribution.

Q: Under Alabama law, how often must a credit union review their records for unclaimed property?

A: Under Alabama’s Unclaimed Property Act, all businesses must review their records each year to determine whether they are in possession of any unclaimed property, to file an annual report of their findings, and to remit the unclaimed property to the Alabama Office of State Treasurer’s Unclaimed Property Division. The report must be filed before November 1 of each year and must cover the 12 months preceding July 1 of that year, and any unclaimed property not reported from the previous years. Any business that does not have unclaimed property to report is not required to file a report in the given report year.

Unclaimed property includes all intangible property (for example, checking and savings accounts, wages, money order, credit balances, refunds, and any other funds payable to a person) and tangible contents of safe deposit boxes. Alabama provides different dormancy periods for different types of property, see link below.

Q: Under Florida law, how long must a credit union wait until it can presume a deposit at their institution is abandoned?

A: A deposit in a financial institution is presumed unclaimed unless, within 5 years, the owner has:

a. Increased or decreased the amount of the deposit or presented the passbook or other similar evidence of the deposit for the crediting of interest;

b. Communicated in writing or by documented telephone contact with the banking or financial organization concerning the property;

c. Otherwise indicated an interest in the property as evidenced by a memorandum or other record on file with the banking or financial organization;

d. Owned other property to which paragraph (a), paragraph (b), or paragraph (c) is applicable and if the banking or financial organization communicates in writing with the owner with regard to the property that would otherwise be presumed unclaimed under this subsection at the address to which communications regarding the other property regularly are sent; or

e. Had another relationship with the banking or financial organization concerning which the owner has: communicated in writing with the banking or financial organization, or otherwise indicated an interest as evidenced by a memorandum or other record on file with the banking or financial organization and if the banking or financial organization communicates in writing with the owner with regard to the property that would otherwise be unclaimed under this subsection at the address to which communications regarding the other relationship regularly are sent. 717.106(1)

Q: Under Georgia law, how long must the holder of any property wait until it can be presumed abandoned?

A: Georgia’s Disposition of Unclaimed Property Act is found at O.C.G.A. 44-12-190 et seq. All tangible and intangible property, including any income or increment thereon, less any lawful charges, that is held, issued, or owing in the ordinary course of the holder's business and has remained unclaimed by the owner for more than five years after it became payable or distributable is presumed abandoned. O.C.G.A. 44-12-193.
